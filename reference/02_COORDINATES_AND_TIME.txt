Current repo = space is fixed, Earth spins.

If you remember only one sentence, remember that.

The Four Things

ECI
Meaning: inertial space frame.
Use here for: satellites, Sun, and the overall scene.
Mental picture: stars and orbital planes make sense here.

ECEF
Meaning: Earth-fixed frame.
Use here for: latitude/longitude, ground positions, “what country is underneath.”
Mental picture: Earth map coordinates.

GMST/GAST
Meaning: the rotation angle between inertial space and Earth-fixed geography.
Use here for: rotating Earth relative to the inertial scene.
Mental picture: “how far has Earth turned since the reference orientation?”

Sun
Meaning: a direction in space, not glued to Earth.
Use here for: lighting in the inertial scene.
Mental picture: the Earth rotates under the sunlight.

This Project’s Actual Model

Scene/world frame: ECI
Satellite positions from worker: ECI/TEME, then axis-swapped into Three.js
Earth globe: a mesh at origin that rotates by sidereal angle every frame
Earth geography: becomes correct because the mesh rotates
Sun direction: computed in ECI, passed directly to shaders
Starfield: visual backdrop, consistent with inertial-space thinking

What GMST/GAST Is Doing Here
Think of GMST/GAST as a conversion angle, not a place.

If you have an inertial scene and want Earth geography to line up:
Rotate Earth by sidereal angle.
If you had an Earth-fixed scene instead:
Keep Earth still, and rotate satellites and Sun into Earth-fixed coordinates.
That is why the HTML plan and the repo differ. They chose opposite scene conventions.

Where Is the Sun?
In this repo, the Sun is in ECI.

That means:

getSunDirection(now) returns an inertial-space direction.
Engine.ts copies that direction into the Earth shaders.
The Earth mesh rotates by getGAST(now).
Result: day/night moves correctly because Earth turns under a fixed Sun.
The key comment is in time.ts:
“No GAST rotation is applied here … Engine rotates the Earth mesh.”

So:

Sun does not get GMST-rotated in the current project.
Earth does.
Never Mix These Up Again
Ask this first:

What is my scene frame?

If answer is ECI:

satellites in ECI
Sun in ECI
Earth rotates by GMST/GAST
If answer is ECEF:

Earth stays still
satellites converted to ECEF
Sun rotated into ECEF for lighting
Pick one. Never blend them halfway.

Tailored Mnemonic
For this repo:

Satellites and Sun live in space. Earth turns to meet them.

That is the whole model.

One Small Naming Note
Your function is called getGAST, but the implementation/comments in time.ts are really serving as the sidereal Earth-rotation angle for rendering. For project thinking, treat it as:

the angle used to rotate Earth in the inertial scene

That will keep you sane even if the astronomy naming gets fuzzy.

RECAP FOR A FRESH RE-CODE (the concrete bits, self-contained)

SCENE UNIT
  1 world unit = 1 Earth radius (ER) = 6,371,000 m = 6371 km.
  So an object's distance from origin is just "radius in ER":
      radius_ER = 1 + altitude_km / 6371
  (Why it matters: clouds/atmosphere are razor-thin in these units — see
  04_DEPTH_ALIGNMENT.txt §1-2. Keep ONE unit everywhere; surface at radius 1.0.)

AXIS SWAP (worker ECI/TEME -> three.js)
  The SGP4 worker gives ECI/TEME (x, y, z). three.js is Y-up, and we treat the
  ECI Z axis (north pole) as three.js Y. The mapping we use:
      three.x =  eci.x
      three.y =  eci.z
      three.z = -eci.y
  Apply the same swap to satellites, the Sun, and anything else coming from the
  inertial frame, so they all share one convention.

WHERE THE TWO ANGLES/DIRS ARE SET (per frame)
  - Earth spin:  earthGroup.rotation.y = getGAST(now)   (the Earth mesh/Group)
  - Sun:         sunDir = getSunDirection(now)  (ECI, already swapped to three.js)
                 -> written into the Earth/surface shaders and (if used) the
                    atmosphere/clouds. ONE sun source for everything (mixing a
                    second ECEF sun source caused a 90-degree double terminator).

ECI <-> ECEF (when a layer needs Earth-fixed)
  ECEF = ECI rotated by -GAST about the polar (scene-Y) axis. Two common needs:
  - Physically-based atmosphere/clouds that work in ECEF metres: feed them
      worldToECEF = Scale(metresPerUnit) * RotY(-GAST)
    (see 06_TAKRAM_ATMOSPHERE.txt). This is the SAME -GAST that spins the Earth,
    just inverted, so everything stays locked.
  - Sampling a surface-fixed (geographic) texture in a shader: take the fragment's
    world radial and remove GAST. Latitude is unaffected by spin:
      lat     = asin(worldRadial.y)
      lon_geo = lon_world - GAST          (lon_world = atan2 of the radial)
    Used for night lights / snow-ice masks that are pinned to geography.

PARENTING RULE (keeps alignment automatic)
  Parent everything "fixed to the ground" (surface tiles, cloud/atmosphere shells,
  observer marker) to the Earth Group that you rotate by GAST. Leave inertial
  things (satellites, Sun, starfield) in the scene root. Then geography lines up
  under the satellites for free, and you never hand-sync rotations.

SOURCE-OF-TRUTH FILES IN THE CURRENT CODE (for cross-checking during a re-code)
  - orbital/time.ts        : getGAST(now), getSunDirection(now)
  - engine/Engine.ts       : sets earthGroup.rotation.y and copies the sun each frame
  - geospatial/CoordinateBridge.ts : ECI->ECEF helpers (sun + camera) for effects
  (Line numbers drift; search by symbol.)
